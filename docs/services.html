Church & Nonprofit Support Services

These services are designed for churches, nonprofits, and community organizations that need dependable help with updates, communication flow, and recurring operational work.

Service options

Three clear ways support can begin

The best first project is usually focused and practical. Most organizations do not need everything at once. They need one useful bottleneck removed first.

Best for churches, nonprofits, and community groups

Monthly Website & Communications Support

Ongoing support to keep public information current, announcements more consistent, and recurring digital updates from falling behind.

  • Website page updates and cleanup
  • Event, service, and program information updates
  • Bulletin or newsletter uploads
  • Announcement and reminder posting support
  • Homepage refreshes and seasonal updates
  • Simple content organization and consistency improvements
Best fit when your website or public communication is important, but updates are not happening as consistently as they should.
Best for smaller active teams

Operations Setup & Workflow Improvement

Practical systems that reduce friction, improve follow-through, and make recurring work easier for your team to manage.

  • Recurring admin checklists
  • Action trackers and decision logs
  • Folder and file organization improvements
  • Shared workflow structure for repeatable tasks
  • Simple intake or handoff process improvements
  • Light AI-enabled workflow support where appropriate
Best fit when important recurring work depends too much on memory, scattered messages, or inconsistent handoff.
Best for a one-time practical project

Starter Website or Website Refresh

Simple website setup or cleanup for organizations that need a more current, better-organized online presence without a large custom build.

  • Small website setup or refresh
  • Page structure cleanup and content organization
  • Service, event, ministry, or contact page updates
  • Homepage messaging improvement
  • Basic launch or relaunch support
  • Limited support for selected local businesses where there is a strong fit
Best fit when the current site feels outdated, unclear, or difficult to keep current, but the need does not require a full agency engagement.

Examples of first projects

Common starting points

Many engagements begin with one of these practical needs.

Website cleanup

Outdated pages, missing details, broken content flow, or public information that needs attention.

Weekly update support

Recurring help for announcements, reminders, bulletin uploads, or event communication.

Event communication setup

A clearer plan for posting event information across the website and public channels.

Recurring admin structure

Checklists, trackers, or shared organization for work that happens every week or month.

Folder and file organization

Better shared structure for documents, recurring files, and team access.

Simple process improvement

A lighter, clearer way to manage routine internal follow-up without overcomplicating the work.

How support can begin

A clear and manageable first step

1

Identify the bottleneck

We start with the area that feels most delayed, inconsistent, or hard to manage.

2

Define a practical scope

The first scope is meant to be clear enough to act on and small enough to be realistic.

3

Build from what works

Once one key area is working better, further support can stay focused and intentional.

Best fit when

You need practical, scoped support

  • Your team is active but stretched
  • Updates matter, but consistency is slipping
  • You want help that is clear and manageable
  • You want dependable support without unnecessary complexity

Better fit elsewhere when

You need a larger agency-style engagement

  • Large advertising or lead-generation campaigns
  • Heavy custom development or software work
  • Complex multi-brand creative programs
  • Extensive agency production teams across many channels
